# Science Fiction Influence on Real Missions

Science fiction has shaped space exploration more profoundly than most acknowledge. Arthur C. Clarke invented the communication satellite in 1945 — 20 years before the first one launched. Star Trek's communicator directly inspired the mobile phone. The Mars Society's Robert Zubrin credited Kim Stanley Robinson's Mars trilogy with building public support for Mars exploration. NASA's Jet Propulsion Laboratory actively recruits from sci-fi fan communities and has collaborated with sci-fi authors through the Innovative Advanced Concepts (NIAC) programme. The pattern: fiction establishes the cultural imagination first, making the engineering case easier to fund. A compelling narrative for Mars settlement — not just visiting — may be the missing ingredient for sustained political commitment.
