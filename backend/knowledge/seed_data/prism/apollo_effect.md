# The Apollo Effect

The Apollo programme remains the most powerful example of how space exploration catalyses societal transformation. Between 1961 and 1972, Apollo generated an estimated 1,800 spin-off technologies, created 400,000 jobs at peak employment, and inspired an entire generation of scientists and engineers. University enrolment in STEM fields surged 50% during the Apollo era. The "Earthrise" photograph taken by Apollo 8 is credited with launching the modern environmental movement — seeing Earth from space fundamentally changed humanity's self-perception. The total Apollo budget ($25.4 billion in 1960s dollars, ~$200B adjusted) returned an estimated $7–14 for every dollar invested through technology transfer alone.
