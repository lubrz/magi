# Exploration as Human Imperative

Throughout history, civilisations that stopped exploring stagnated. The Chinese treasure fleet under Zheng He (1405–1433) was the most advanced naval force in the world — then the Ming Dynasty turned inward, burning the fleet, and China fell behind Europe technologically for centuries. The Polynesian settlement of the Pacific — navigating thousands of miles of open ocean with no instruments — demonstrates that exploration is not a modern luxury but a deep human drive. Counter-argument: the Age of Exploration also brought colonialism, exploitation, and ecological devastation. Space exploration must learn from this history — the Outer Space Treaty (1967) attempts to prevent colonial dynamics by declaring space the "province of all mankind."
