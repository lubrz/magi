# Public Perception of Space

Public support for space exploration is consistently wide but shallow. Polling shows ~70% of Americans believe NASA is a good use of federal funds, but only ~25% support increasing NASA's budget. The general public dramatically overestimates NASA's budget share — the average guess is 6% of federal spending; the actual figure is ~0.5%. Media coverage of space is event-driven: launches, landings, and failures dominate. Sustained long-duration missions like ISS operations receive minimal coverage despite continuous scientific output. The implication: major space programmes need periodic "spectacle moments" to maintain public and political support.
