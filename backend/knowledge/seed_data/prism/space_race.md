# Space Race Cold War Dynamics

The Space Race (1957–1975) was fundamentally a proxy conflict — the USSR and USA competed in space to demonstrate ideological superiority without direct military confrontation. Sputnik's launch in 1957 triggered genuine public panic in the US and led to the creation of NASA, DARPA, and the National Defense Education Act. The pattern is instructive: national prestige and competitive fear drove investment levels that pure scientific merit alone could never justify. Today's emerging space race between the US, China, and commercial entities (SpaceX, Blue Origin) follows similar dynamics. China's Chang'e programme and planned lunar base represent a direct challenge, potentially rekindling the political will for ambitious missions.
