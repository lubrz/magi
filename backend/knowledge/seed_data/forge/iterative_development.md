# Iterative vs Waterfall Development

Space engineering is transitioning from waterfall (sequential design-build-test) to iterative development. Traditional approach: 10+ years of design, review, manufacturing, then a single high-stakes test flight (SLS model). Iterative approach: rapid prototyping, frequent testing, accept failures as learning (SpaceX model). SpaceX's Starship programme has destroyed multiple vehicles during testing — each failure yielded data that would have taken years of simulation. The iterative model requires tolerance for public failures and lower per-test costs. It works best when hardware is relatively inexpensive compared to engineering time. For human-rated systems, a hybrid approach may be optimal: iterate rapidly on propulsion and structures, then apply rigorous waterfall verification for life-critical systems.
