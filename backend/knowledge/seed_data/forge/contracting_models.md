# Cost-Plus vs Fixed-Price Contracting

NASA's contracting model profoundly affects programme outcomes. Cost-plus contracts (used for SLS, Orion) reimburse the contractor for all costs plus a guaranteed profit margin — creating perverse incentives to overrun. Fixed-price contracts (used for Commercial Crew with SpaceX/Boeing) set a firm price, putting schedule and cost risk on the contractor. Results speak clearly: Commercial Crew delivered Dragon Crew for $2.6 billion; Boeing's Starliner (also fixed-price but with a higher bid) has overrun by $1.5+ billion absorbed by Boeing. The Commercial Cargo programme saved NASA an estimated 90% versus traditional contracting. However, fixed-price only works when the technology is mature enough for contractors to estimate risk. Truly novel systems (like nuclear propulsion) may require cost-plus initially.
