# NASA Budget Constraints

NASA's annual budget has remained remarkably stable at approximately 0.4–0.5% of the US federal budget since the 1990s (~$25 billion in 2024). During Apollo, NASA received up to 4.4% of the federal budget. This 10x reduction in relative funding fundamentally constrains mission architecture. A crewed Mars mission is estimated at $100–500 billion over 20 years — 4–20 years of NASA's entire budget. This means Mars requires either: massive budget increases (politically unlikely), international cost-sharing (complex), commercial partnerships (SpaceX model), or phased approach over decades. The ISS consumed ~$3–4 billion annually for operations alone, limiting funds for exploration. Decommissioning ISS (planned ~2030) would free significant resources but creates a gap in low-Earth orbit presence.
