# Artemis Programme Cost Analysis

NASA's Artemis programme to return humans to the Moon has experienced significant cost escalation. The Space Launch System (SLS) development exceeded $23 billion — more than double the original estimate. Each SLS launch costs approximately $2.2 billion (non-reusable), compared to SpaceX Falcon Heavy at $150 million (partially reusable) and projected Starship costs of $10–50 million (fully reusable). The Orion capsule added another $20+ billion in development costs. NASA's Inspector General estimated total Artemis costs through Artemis III at $93 billion. The cost-per-kilogram-to-orbit for SLS is roughly $58,000/kg versus Falcon Heavy's $1,500/kg — a 40x difference. The programme demonstrates the risks of cost-plus contracting and requirements creep in government space programmes.
