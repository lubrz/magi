# SpaceX Starship Development

SpaceX's Starship represents the most ambitious launch vehicle in development. Designed for full reusability with 150+ tonnes to LEO capacity, it aims to reduce launch costs by 100x compared to existing systems. The iterative "test, fail, learn" development approach contrasts sharply with traditional aerospace — SpaceX destroyed multiple prototypes during rapid testing rather than spending years on paper studies. Total development costs estimated at $5–10 billion (privately funded), compared to SLS's $23+ billion (government-funded). Key engineering risks: orbital refuelling (requires 6–8 tanker flights per mission), heat shield reliability for Earth return, and life support for deep-space missions. Starship's success would fundamentally change the economics of space exploration — but it remains unproven for crewed lunar or Mars missions.
