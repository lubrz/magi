# Radiation Exposure

Beyond Earth's magnetosphere, astronauts face two primary radiation hazards: galactic cosmic rays (GCR) and solar particle events (SPE). GCR delivers a continuous dose of approximately 0.5–1.0 mSv per day in interplanetary space. A 6-month Mars transit exposes crews to roughly 300 mSv — compared to the annual limit of 20 mSv for radiation workers on Earth. Cumulative mission dose for a Mars round-trip could exceed 1 Sv, significantly increasing lifetime cancer risk. Shielding options include polyethylene-based materials, water walls, and electromagnetic field generators, but all add mass. The Lunar Gateway's orbit outside the Van Allen belts will provide valuable long-duration radiation data.
