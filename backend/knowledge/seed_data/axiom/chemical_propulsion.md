# Chemical Propulsion Systems

Chemical propulsion remains the most proven and widely used technology for space launch and orbital manoeuvres. Liquid bipropellant engines (LOX/LH2, LOX/RP-1) deliver specific impulse values of 350–450 seconds in vacuum. Solid rocket boosters provide high thrust-to-weight ratios but cannot be throttled or shut down once ignited. The Space Shuttle Main Engine (RS-25) achieved 452 seconds Isp, representing near-theoretical maximum for chemical propulsion. The fundamental limitation is the energy density of chemical bonds — no chemical reaction can exceed approximately 500 seconds Isp, establishing a hard ceiling on performance.
