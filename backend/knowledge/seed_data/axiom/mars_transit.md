# Mars Transit Time

The minimum-energy transfer orbit (Hohmann transfer) from Earth to Mars requires approximately 9 months of travel time one-way, with launch windows occurring every 26 months due to orbital alignment. Total mission duration for a conjunction-class Mars mission is approximately 900 days (including 500 days on the surface). Opposition-class missions reduce surface time but increase total transit time and radiation exposure. Nuclear thermal propulsion could reduce one-way transit to 4–6 months, while advanced nuclear electric propulsion might achieve 3–4 months. Shorter transit times directly reduce crew radiation exposure — a critical health constraint.
