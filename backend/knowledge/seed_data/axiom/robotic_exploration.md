# Robotic Exploration

Robotic missions have been extraordinarily successful in planetary exploration. Mars rovers (Spirit, Opportunity, Curiosity, Perseverance) have operated for years beyond design life, with Opportunity lasting 15 years. The cost of Perseverance ($2.7B) is roughly 1/50th of estimated crewed Mars mission costs. Robotic probes can survive environments lethal to humans — Venus surface (Venera), Jupiter radiation belts (Juno), deep space (Voyagers). However, robots lack human adaptability — a geologist on Mars could accomplish in one hour what a rover does in a week. The key trade-off is efficiency per dollar versus flexibility and speed of discovery.
