# Nuclear Thermal Propulsion

Nuclear Thermal Propulsion (NTP) uses a nuclear fission reactor to heat hydrogen propellant to extreme temperatures before expelling it through a nozzle. Theoretical Isp of 850–1000 seconds — roughly double chemical propulsion. NASA's NERVA programme (1960s–1973) successfully tested multiple NTP engines on the ground, achieving 841 seconds Isp with the Pewee reactor. The technology was proven but cancelled due to budget cuts and shifting political priorities. Modern NTP concepts (DRACO programme with DARPA) aim to demonstrate an NTP system in orbit by the late 2020s. Key challenges: nuclear safety during launch, reactor shielding mass, and regulatory approvals for nuclear materials in space.
