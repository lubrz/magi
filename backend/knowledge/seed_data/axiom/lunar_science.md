# Lunar Science Value

The Moon offers unique scientific opportunities that cannot be replicated on Earth or Mars. The permanently shadowed craters at the poles contain water ice deposits confirmed by LCROSS impact data and the Chandrayaan missions — potentially billions of tonnes. The lunar far side provides the only radio-quiet environment in the inner solar system, ideal for low-frequency radio astronomy shielded from Earth's electromagnetic noise. Lunar regolith preserves a 4.5 billion-year record of solar activity, asteroid impacts, and galactic cosmic ray flux. The Moon also serves as a testbed for in-situ resource utilisation (ISRU) technologies — extracting oxygen from regolith, producing water from ice — that are essential precursors to Mars missions.
